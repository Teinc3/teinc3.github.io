## Malaysian Politics
*Last updated: 30/06/2026*
*Genre: History & Politics*

*Disclaimer: I am not Malaysian, no second degree family relative of mine is malaysian 
(can't verify third and above, there probably is one that I'm not aware of).
I just find this interesting, that's really it.*

How do I summarise Malaysian politics in one sentence? Pragmatic racism.
When you see american conservatives crying over DEI and affirmative action for minorities, only then
you realise that it is explicitly written into the fucking **Constitution** of another country.

Sometimes I wish to just put a bland libertarian lens on this country, such as "why not let the 
Chinese leave then, it'll crash the economy and you'll have a shithole left for affirmative action".
But through a pragmatic lense I also see how it's *not* possible. At least in the 60s and 70s, when 
racism was definitely way more prevalent across the west, Mainland China was still a shithole. 
There's not really anywhere else the Chinese can go. Maybe Japan with their booming economy, but I 
doubt the Malaysian Chinese who witnessed the Japanese invasion just 20 years ago would enjoy the
prospect of migrating to a country they view as an aggressor and destroyer of their prior homeland.
At least the Afrikaan and Rhodesian colonisers who wanted to leave were wealthy enough to do so. 
(Trump with his refugee programme targetting exclusively south africans is kinda funny honestly)

My opinion is that during the conception of the Federation of Malaya (and subsequently, Malaysia) 
there was one thing that the MCA did absolutely right and it held support for Tunku's Alliance
instead of LKY's "Malaysian Malaysia" policy. However well the ethnic minority vote coalesce the 
Malays will always get a majority, so any Malaysian Malaysia policy will simply be swamped in 
ethnic tension, conflicts and more. Fundamentally, I agree with LKY fundamentally in the efficacy of
meritocracy, and that special rights for bumis is literally a textbook definition of affirmative
action and hurts the economy (as we've seen), fighting an unwinnable ethnic war with a lurking 
Indonesia nextdoors wanting to annex everyone is even worse (Konfrontasi). And in exchange for
"special rights", you get the "social contract" that legalises every immgirant as a citizen with
full voting rights. When the Malaysian Chinese community have nowhere to go except staying in
Malaysia, and any other option involving ethnic tensions, civil war and communist guerillas will 
inevitably lead to Indonesian dominance (and Suharto proved it would have been a bad idea).

Of course in big 2026 everything has changed. Reverse brain drain of talent and low birth rates of
Chinese and Indians altered the demographics so much that it is no longer a threat to Malays. And it
kinda is expected when you run an affirmative action policy over a period of 50 years. Now Singapore
by capita is idek how many times richer than Malaysia. It's like the classic meme of socialism 
making everyone poor while capitalism makes someone insanely rich and others moderately well-off.
But compare Malaysia's economy to Indonesia's and you instantly know that MCA made a smart decision 
back then (Not saying it is making good ones now, they aren't even relevant nowadays tbf).
